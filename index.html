Shanghai, China

I am an undergraduate student in Biomedical Engineering at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, advised by Prof. Xiaoyun Yuan at the OpticAI Lab, AI Institute, SJTU. You can also call me Theo.

My research lies at the intersection of generative AI, computational photography, and low-level vision. I am broadly interested in image restoration and enhancement — spanning night photography deflaring, compression artifact removal, and human-aware recovery — with a focus on leveraging diffusion models, semantic guidance, and perceptual quality optimization.

I have (co-)first-authored papers accepted at NeurIPS 2025, AAAI 2026, and SIGGRAPH 2026, covering topics from night photography enhancement and JPEG artifact removal to human-aware image restoration with one-step diffusion.

selected publications

  1. SIGGRAPH
    LUCID: Learning Unified Control for Image Deflaring and Exposure Mastery in Nighttime Photography
    Tingyu Yang
    In ACM SIGGRAPH 2026 Conference Papers, 2026
    Abs

    We present LUCID, a unified controllable image restoration framework for nighttime photography that jointly handles lens flare removal and exposure adjustment. By combining flare decoupling with Diffusion CFG control, LUCID achieves continuous brightness adjustment through mixed training and supports text-guided light source preservation, enabling multi-mode training and multi-effect inference within a single model. The framework also leverages multi-exposure scene reconstruction for high-quality nighttime HDR synthesis, achieving state-of-the-art IQA metrics on large-scale real-world nighttime datasets.

  2. AAAI
    SODiff: Semantic-Oriented Diffusion Model for JPEG Compression Artifacts Removal
    Tingyu Yang* and others
    In Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence, 2026
    Abs arXiv

    We propose SODiff, an image restoration method for JPEG compression artifact removal that aligns image features with semantic guidance to better exploit pretrained generative priors. By bridging compressed image features with semantic information, SODiff improves texture reconstruction naturalness and overall perceptual quality under high compression ratios.

  3. NeurIPS
    HAODiff: Human-Aware One-Step Diffusion via Dual-Prompt Guidance
    Tingyu Yang* and others
    In Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 2025
    Abs arXiv

    HAODiff addresses image restoration for human subjects under complex degradation conditions. By integrating human-aware priors with conditional guidance mechanisms, it enhances the recovery of motion-blurred body regions, maintaining perceptual quality and structural fidelity in challenging real-world degradation scenarios.
